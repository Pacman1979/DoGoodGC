# JD's WOW Factor Marketing System

John Dwyer ("JD") is one of Australia's most successful direct-response marketers. His WOW Attraction Formula has generated over $15 billion in extra sales across 27,000+ businesses. He convinced Jerry Seinfeld (who had only done 2 other ads — Amex & Microsoft) to be spokesperson for The Greater Building Society, tripling their home loan market share in 24 months. His core philosophy: **"In a sea of sameness, you need to stand out like a beacon."**

---

## JD's Core Philosophy (Apply to Everything)

1. **Sell on VALUE, never on price.** Competing on price is a race to the bottom and is unsustainable. The goal is to take the customer's eyes OFF the price and onto a "wow factor."
2. **Direct-response over brand-building.** Every marketing dollar must be measurable and drive a specific action. Pretty logos and nice colours don't pay the bills.
3. **Become the "UN" of your industry.** Like Virgin Airlines — Richard Branson had the same planes as everyone else, but positioned himself as UNlike anyone else. You need a marketing advantage if you don't have a product advantage (Apple) or systems advantage (McDonald's).
4. **Agitate the problem, then offer the solution.** People pay to get rid of pain more than they pay to gain pleasure. Problem → Aggravate → Solve.
5. **Your website IS your Director of First Impressions.** 99% of prospects check your website first. It must SELL, not just look good.

## STEP 1: Identify the Most Profitable Customer (WHO, WHY, WHERE)

Before touching copy or design, get crystal clear on the ideal client:

- Be hyper-specific. Not "women" — "a woman, 25–34, 2 children, white-collar job, upper-class suburb."
- Ask: Who spends the most? Who stays the longest? Who refers others?
- Find more people who look exactly like your best existing clients.
- Once you know WHO they are, you know WHERE to find them (Facebook targeting, local media, community groups, etc.).

---

## STEP 2: The WOW Factor (Incentive-Based Marketing)

The WOW Factor is a compelling incentive that makes prospects choose YOU over competitors — without lowering your price.

**Classic JD examples:**
- "Get a home loan → receive a free holiday." (Jerry Seinfeld / Greater Building Society)
- Fuel savings cards, free vacations, experiences — things the customer perceives as high value but cost the business far less.
- "Give away your own product as a prize" on Facebook — generates hyper-qualified leads because only genuine prospects enter.

**Creating a WOW Factor:**
- What can you offer that competitors don't even think about?
- What does your ideal client genuinely desire beyond your core product?
- The incentive must feel EXTRAORDINARY, not a standard discount.
- Think: free reports, exclusive guides, loyalty rewards, experiential bonuses.

## STEP 3: The WOW Homepage Formula (8 Must-Have Components)

JD treats the homepage like the front cover of a magazine — the most important real estate in the business. A homepage must SELL, not just inform.

### The 8 Components (in order):

**1. Problem/Solution Headline (Above the Fold)**
- Must immediately capture attention and evoke intrigue.
- Formula: Identify the #1 pain point of the ideal client → frame the headline as the solution.
- Example: "Tired of Paying Too Much for Energy? Here's How Gold Coast Businesses Are Slashing Bills by 40%"
- Must make the prospect think: "That's talking to ME."

**2. Persuasive Subheadline / Explanatory Copy**
- Expand on the headline with copy that sends "chilling thoughts" through the prospect's mind.
- Agitate the problem. Make them feel the pain of NOT acting.
- Use emotional language — fear of loss is more powerful than promise of gain.

**3. Reinforcing Images**
- Images that "nail down" the message. Visuals must reinforce the copy, not just look decorative.
- Show the PROBLEM or the SOLUTION visually — before/after, people experiencing the pain, people enjoying the result.

**4. Welcome Video (Critical)**
- A short video where the business owner (or a representative) explains:
  - What problem they solve
  - Who they serve
  - What the visitor should do next (CTA)
- If camera-shy: use a doodle/whiteboard animation video.
- Video builds trust faster than any other medium.

**5. Benefits via Images + Copy**
- Expand on the benefits of choosing this business.
- Use dot points if needed, but frame each as a benefit to the customer, not a feature of the product.
- "What's in it for ME?" thinking at all times.

**6. Call to Action + Data Capture (Lead Magnet)**
- Every homepage must capture visitor data — most won't buy on first visit.
- Offer something irresistible for FREE in exchange for name/email: a guide, a report, a checklist, a quote tool.
- Integrate with a CRM (Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, Infusionsoft, etc.).
- The goal: turn anonymous visitors into known leads you can follow up.

**7. Video Testimonials**
- Not just written quotes — VIDEO testimonials of real clients.
- These are the most powerful conversion tool on any website.
- JD's system: capture them at the moment clients are MOST HAPPY (just after a win/result).
- Show real faces, real names, real results.

**8. More Compelling Reasons to Choose You**
- Social proof, credentials, media appearances, awards, number of clients served.
- Overcome any remaining objections.
- Reassure the prospect they are making the right choice.

## STEP 4: Theming (Turn "Bland" into "Grand")

JD is famous for "theming" businesses to make them stand out dramatically. This goes beyond just a logo refresh.

- Give the business a personality, a character, a story.
- Example: A boring "Educational Opal Mine Tour" was re-themed as an Indiana Jones-style family adventure — revenue skyrocketed immediately.
- Ask: What THEME could make this business instantly more exciting, more memorable, more shareable?
- Think Disney. Think entertainment. Think: would a child find this exciting?
- The theme must match the target audience (upmarket = BMW feel; working class = Kmart feel).

## STEP 5: The 10-Point WOW Marketing System (Full Business Plan)

Beyond the website, JD's complete system covers:

1. **WHO, WHY, WHERE** — Define the most profitable customer precisely.
2. **WOW Factor** — Create a compelling incentive that takes eyes off the price.
3. **Emotional Direct Response** — Use emotion-first copywriting across all channels.
4. **Fix the Website** — Apply the 8-component homepage formula.
5. **Hold Events** — People love events; use them to attract and convert leads at scale.
6. **Video Testimonials** — Systematically capture and deploy client video testimonials.
7. **Get Free Publicity** — Become a storyteller; pitch media with interesting angles.
8. **Social Media with Incentives** — Facebook/Instagram contests, giveaways, lead magnets. Never just "post content" — always attach an incentive.
9. **Database Building** — Build and nurture a list. The money is in the follow-up.
10. **Repetitive Trade / Loyalty** — Get clients to return over and over. Turn customers into raving fans who refer others.

## STEP 6: Facebook / Social Media (JD's Approach)

- Never run a contest where you give away something unrelated (e.g., an iPhone). You'll attract prize-seekers, not real customers.
- **Give away YOUR OWN product or service as the prize.** Only genuine prospects enter.
- Target hyper-specifically using Facebook/Instagram's demographic and interest tools.
- Always attach an incentive to every post/ad — never just "content marketing" alone.
- JD achieved 1,000 leads in a week for $20/day Facebook spend using this method.

## STEP 7: Copy Principles (Write Like JD)

When writing copy for any channel (web, ads, email, social):

- **Lead with the problem.** Never lead with "Welcome to our website."
- **Use "you" and "your" constantly** — make it about the reader, not the business.
- **Short sentences. Short paragraphs.** Scannable. Punchy.
- **Use ellipses and dashes** to build pace and urgency.
- **Avoid jargon.** Speak like a human, not a corporate.
- **End every section with a micro-CTA** — tell them what to do next.
- **Use specifics, not generalities.** "40% cheaper" beats "save money."
- **Urgency and scarcity** — give people a reason to act NOW.

## How to Use This Skill: Website Redesign Workflow

When a user wants to redesign a website using JD's methodology, follow this process:

### Phase 1: Intake (Ask These Questions)
1. What does the business do? What problem do they solve?
2. Who is their ideal/most profitable customer? Be specific.
3. What makes them different from competitors? (Current USP, if any)
4. What action do they want visitors to take on the homepage?
5. Do they have video testimonials? A welcome video?
6. Do they have a lead magnet / free offer?
7. What is their current headline on the homepage?
8. Can they share the current website URL for review?

### Phase 2: Critique (Apply JD's Lens)
Review the current site against the 8-component homepage formula. For each component, note: Present / Missing / Weak.

### Phase 3: Redesign Recommendations
Provide specific, actionable recommendations for:
- New headline (problem/solution formula)
- Copy improvements
- Video suggestions
- Lead magnet ideas
- WOW Factor / incentive ideas
- Theming suggestions if relevant
- CTA placement and wording

### Phase 4: Write the Copy
Write actual copy for the homepage sections if requested, applying JD's direct-response principles throughout.
